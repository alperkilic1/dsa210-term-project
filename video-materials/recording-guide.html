Recording Guide

How to turn the slide deck into a 2–3 minute narrated video. The video is optional supplementary material — not a required submission.

ARecommended method — PowerPoint "Record Slide Show"

Everything stays inside one tool, so there are no screen-recording sync issues.

  1. Open Incident-Genome-Slides.pptx in PowerPoint for Mac.
  2. Go to Slide Show → Record Slide Show.
  3. Narrate each slide and advance manually — PowerPoint records audio and timings per slide. The narration is already in each slide's speaker notes.
  4. When done: File → Export → Create a Video. Choose 720p (HD), keep "Use Recorded Timings and Narrations" on. It saves as .mp4.
You can re-record a single slide without redoing the whole deck — handy if one take goes wrong.

BBackup method — QuickTime screen recording

If PowerPoint's recorder misbehaves (it sometimes does on macOS):

  1. Open the slides in Slideshow mode.
  2. Press Cmd+Shift+5 and choose "Record Entire Screen".
  3. Click Options and set the microphone to your input device.
  4. Start recording, present the slides, then stop. It saves a .mov to the Desktop.

CMicrophone tips

  • The built-in mic is fine for 2–3 minutes — just sit in a quiet room and close the window.
  • EarPods or AirPods work even better; the mic sits closer to your mouth.
  • Test first: record 10 seconds, play it back, listen for echo or fan noise.
  • Quit heavy apps before recording so the laptop fan stays quiet.

DKeeping the file small

A 2-minute narrated slide video usually lands at 1–3 MB with the settings below. If yours is bigger, compress it with ffmpeg:

ffmpeg -i presentation.mp4 -vcodec libx264 -crf 28 -preset slow \
  -vf scale=1280:720 -acodec aac -b:a 96k -movflags +faststart \
  output_compressed.mp4
  • CRF 28 is visually fine for slides and dramatically smaller.
  • 720p video + 96k audio is more than enough for narrated slides.

FPre-record checklist

  • Script rehearsed once out loud, timed under 3 minutes.
  • Slides finalized — no "TODO" placeholders visible.
  • Quiet room, window closed, fan-heavy apps quit.
  • Mic input verified (System Settings → Sound → Input — watch the level meter).
  • Do Not Disturb ON — no notification sounds mid-recording.
  • 10-second test recording played back — audio clear, no clipping.
  • Phone on silent.
